Services & Common Pests

Clear, professional service — no gimmicks. Call the office closest to you. State-licensed to provide pest control service.

Termites

Termites can cause serious structural damage and often remain hidden until damage is advanced. If you’ve seen swarmers, mud tubes, or damaged wood, schedule an evaluation quickly so we can identify risk areas, contributing conditions (like moisture and wood-to-soil contact), and practical next steps.

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs hide in mattress seams, furniture joints, baseboards, and other tight spaces. Successful control depends on preparation and a complete approach that targets all hiding places and life stages. Use the prep sheet below before service.

Roaches

Roaches can contaminate surfaces and may trigger allergies. Kitchens, bathrooms, and utility areas are common hotspots. Good outcomes usually require sanitation, reducing moisture, and access to key areas around sinks, appliances, and plumbing penetrations. Use the roach prep sheet below before service.

Fleas

Flea issues often involve more than what you see—eggs, larvae, and pupae can be present in carpets and pet areas. We’ll advise on coordinated steps for the home, pets, and environment to help break the life cycle and prevent reinfestation.

Preparation Sheets

These steps help treatments work better and faster. Print if you want a checklist.

Bed Bug Prep Sheet

Goal: Provide access to all likely hiding areas so treatment reaches bed bug harborages.

Before service (recommended):

  • Declutter floors: remove loose items so baseboards and furniture edges are accessible.
  • Closets & drawers: remove items from closets, nightstands, dressers, and similar furniture as instructed when scheduling.
  • Linens: remove all bedding from beds (sheets, comforters, pillowcases) and bag it for transport to the washer.
  • Laundry: wash and dry on the hottest settings allowed by the fabric label; keep cleaned items sealed/bagged until the room is cleared.
  • Vacuum: vacuum mattress seams, bed frames, headboards, and baseboards; seal/discard vacuum contents immediately.
  • Do NOT move items room-to-room: this spreads bed bugs into clean areas.
  • Furniture access: pull beds and nightstands slightly away from walls if you can do so safely.
  • Encasements (if recommended): be prepared to install bed bug-rated mattress/box spring encasements if directed.

Day of service:

  • Follow any instructions given by your technician for pets, occupants, access, and re-entry.
  • Keep kids and pets away from treated areas until cleared by the technician.

After service:

  • Do not deep-clean treated cracks/crevices unless instructed (this can reduce effectiveness).
  • Continue laundering and reducing clutter to prevent reintroduction.
  • Follow up as directed—bed bug control may require multiple steps depending on severity and layout.
Roach Prep Sheet

Goal: Reduce food/water sources and provide access to key areas so treatment reaches main harborages.

Before service (recommended):

  • Sanitation: clean counters and cooking surfaces; wipe grease around stove/backsplash.
  • Food storage: store food in sealed containers; reduce crumbs and standing dishes overnight.
  • Trash: empty trash and use a lidded bin.
  • Under sinks: clear items so plumbing penetrations and corners are accessible.
  • Appliances: clear around stove, refrigerator, dishwasher, and microwave areas if possible.
  • Moisture: fix leaks if possible—roaches thrive where water is available.
  • Pets: don’t leave pet food out overnight; clean pet feeding areas.

After service:

  • Follow your technician’s instructions on when/where to clean—avoid scrubbing treated cracks/crevices immediately unless told.
  • Continue sanitation and moisture control to reduce future activity.
  • Report what you see over the next 1–2 weeks so adjustments can be made if needed.
