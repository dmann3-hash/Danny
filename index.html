Services & Common Pests

Clear, professional service — no gimmicks. Call the office closest to you. State-licensed to provide pest control service.

Termites

Termites can silently damage framing, sill plates, subfloors, and structural wood. We provide inspections, recommendations, and treatment options tailored to the property. If you’re seeing swarmers or mud tubes, call right away.

Bed Bugs

Bed bugs hide in seams, furniture joints, baseboards, and wall voids. Effective control depends on correct preparation and a complete approach that targets all life stages. Look for bites, dark spotting on bedding, and bugs hiding near headboards, seams, and furniture joints. Use the prep sheet below before treatment.

Roaches

Roaches contaminate surfaces and can trigger allergies. Kitchens, bathrooms, and utility rooms are common hotspots. Good outcomes require sanitation, removing food sources, and access to key areas. Common signs include droppings, odor, and activity at night near sinks, fridges, and wall voids. Use the roach prep sheet below before service.

Fleas

Fleas often show up in carpets, pet bedding, and shaded yard areas. Control means breaking the lifecycle: adults, larvae, and eggs. We’ll advise on coordinated steps for the home, pets, and environment. They thrive where pets rest—pet bedding, carpets, and shaded areas outdoors.

Preparation Sheets

These steps help treatments work better and faster. Print if you want a checklist.

Bed Bug Prep Sheet

Before service (recommended):

  • Wash bedding, clothing, and soft items from affected rooms in hot water, then dry on high heat.
  • Bag cleaned items in sealed bags and keep them separated from untreated items.
  • Reduce clutter: clear items from floors and under beds so technicians can access baseboards and furniture edges.
  • Vacuum mattress seams, bed frames, and baseboards. Immediately dispose of the vacuum contents in a sealed bag.
  • Do not move infested items to other rooms (this spreads bed bugs).
  • Ensure technicians can access beds, nightstands, sofas, and typical harborages.

Day of service:

  • Follow any instructions given by your technician for pets, occupants, and access.
  • Keep kids and pets away from treated areas until cleared by the technician.
Roach Prep Sheet

Before service (recommended):

  • Clean kitchen counters and cooking surfaces. Remove grease buildup where possible.
  • Store food in sealed containers; reduce crumbs and standing dishes overnight.
  • Empty trash and use a lidded bin.
  • Clear space under sinks and around appliances so technicians can access plumbing penetrations and harborages.
  • Fix leaks if possible—moisture drives roach activity.

After service:

  • Follow your technician’s instructions on cleaning or avoiding certain areas for a period of time.
  • Continue sanitation to reduce food/water sources and improve long-term results.
